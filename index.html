Background

LINE GO is a ride-hailing service integrated into the LINE app, which is used by over 90% of Taiwan's population. Launched in 2017, it offers ride hailing, car rental, and airport pickup. For ride hailing service, it provides two ride-hailing modes:

üí¨ Chatbot-based Hailing

üéØ Motivations

As a frequent taxi user, I value LINE GO's unique chatbot feature for easy ride bookings and its integration with LINE PAY for seamless payments. Despite these benefits, I've noticed it's underutilized among my peers, leading me to investigate what drives user choices in ride-hailing services.

Moving forward, I will conduct research, interviews, and surveys to explore this issue and further apply the findings to optimize LINE GO's services, identify areas for improvement, propose design solutions, establish success metrics, and create a comprehensive redesign plan.

Takeaways

Key learnings and insights from the design process

üéØ Avoid Personal Bias

I unintentionally brought in my personal bias during the research, prioritizing coupons in taxi-hailing services. Consequently, I focused extensively on coupon-related questions for both the survey and interviews. However, I later realized that coupons were not the primary concern for every taxi user. Unfortunately, I had already invested significant time in this, which turned out to be ineffective.

Learning Point: Maintain objectivity in research design and avoid projecting personal experiences and preferences onto research questions. Consider implementing a bias checklist before starting research.

üìä Use Quantitative Research to Support Qualitative Research

For this project, I conducted the survey before the interviews. Initially, my limited knowledge of LINE GO led me to prepare broad questions, resulting in a lengthy survey that yielded similar results to the interviews without deeper insights. If I had interviewed users first, I could have identified some key issues and crafted targeted survey questions for further exploration.

Learning Point: Recommend starting with small-scale qualitative research to identify key issues, then designing targeted quantitative surveys for validation and expansion. This approach would enable more effective combination of both research methods.

üöÄ Future Improvement Directions

üìã Research Methodology Optimization
  • ‚Ä¢ Establish more systematic research processes
  • ‚Ä¢ Design bias checking mechanisms
  • ‚Ä¢ Optimize combination of qualitative and quantitative research
  • ‚Ä¢ Add user testing validation phases
üé® Design Process Improvement
  • ‚Ä¢ Strengthen technical feasibility assessment
  • ‚Ä¢ Establish design system consistency
  • ‚Ä¢ Enhance business value demonstration
  • ‚Ä¢ Continuous iteration optimization mechanisms
üéì Key Learning

The biggest takeaway from this project is understanding that design is not just about creating beautiful interfaces, but more importantly about understanding real needs and pain points through deep user research. Every design decision should be supported by data and user insights, rather than based on assumptions or personal preferences.
